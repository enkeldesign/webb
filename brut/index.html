Enkel Design by Erik Jansson

Certified specialist, educator, and coach in UX design and digital accessibility.

About Me

I work as an IT development coach and educator at Bolagsverket (the Swedish Companies Registration Office). I assist our development teams in creating accessible and usable digital services for the web. I am also responsible for methodologies and tools for UX design and digital accessibility.

My academic background is in Cognitive Science (Master's degree) and Theoretical Philosophy (Bachelor's degree). In practice my expertise lies in problem-solving and design where people and digital services meet.

Born in 1981 in Stockholm, with family roots in Northern Sweden. Since 2016, I live in Söråker on a farm by a lake with my wife, two children, three cats, and zero mice. I enjoy playing chess and table hockey. I've been involved in web development as a hobby since the 1990s.

Enkel Design in brief

Enkel is a Swedish word with several meanings – among them "simple", "clear" and "uncomplicated". Enkel design is intuitive and easy to understand. It is accessible to all users and purposeful without being complicated.

Enkel design is both aesthetically pleasing and enjoyable to use. Simply a great user experience.
